Quick introduction to ESG

What is ESG?

ESG stands for Environmental, Social, and Governance.

These are the three key factors when measuring the sustainability and ethical impact of an investment in a business or company.

It is a set of standards for a firmâ€™s operations that investors use to screen potential investments for their impact on these critical areas.

Why taking care of ESG is important?
  • Risk Management - Proper ESG practices can help a company anticipate and manage business risks, improving resilience.
  • Regulatory Compliance - Abiding by ESG standards can help a company stay compliant with increasing regulations aimed at sustainability and social responsibility.
  • Market Reputation - Businesses that prioritize ESG usually have a better public image, which can increase customer and investor loyalty.
  • Long-Term Success - ESG targets often align with business sustainability, contributing to the business's long-term success and profitability.
  • Positive Impact - Attention to ESG principles helps businesses contribute positively to society and the environment, avoiding harmful impacts and contributing to global goals like the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals.
What can happen if you don't take ESG into consideration?
  • Legal and Regulatory Penalties - Non-compliance with regulations related to environment, social responsibilities, and governance can result in severe penalties, legal issues, or fines.
  • Reputational Damage - Poor ESG practices can result in a bad public image, leading to loss of customers or decreased loyalty.
  • Adverse Impact on Society and Environment - A lack of ESG consideration might result in harmful outcomes for communities and the environment, such as pollution, unfair labor practices, or unethical actions.
  • Sustainability Challenges - Ignoring ESG practices can also undermine a business's sustainability in the long term, with inadequate preparation for foreseeable changes in the market, environment, and regulatory landscape.
  • Operational Costs - Inefficient use of resources can inflate operating costs and waste valuable assets.
